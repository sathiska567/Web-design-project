Mexican school registration is the process through which students are enrolled in educational institutions in Mexico. The registration process usually takes place at the beginning of the school year and requires parents or legal guardians to submit various documents, including the student's birth certificate, proof of residence, and immunization records. In Mexico, the education system is divided into three levels: basic education, which includes preschool, primary, and secondary education; upper-secondary education; and tertiary education, which includes technical and higher education. Each level has different requirements for school registration. For basic education, registration is mandatory for all children between the ages of 3 and 15. Students are assigned to schools based on their place of residence, and parents can choose between public or private schools. Upper-secondary education is optional and is offered in both public and private institutions. Tertiary education in Mexico includes technical schools, universities, and other higher education institutions.
